Egg Fried Rice

Egg fried rice is one of the most simple dishes you can make, but the correct techniques can make it something truly special. Whether you're trying to get dinner on the table in ten minutes, or you're just craving a late night snack, egg fried rice is truly a staple of comfort food.

You can be as creative or as simple as you want when it comes to this blank canvas. Add in your favorite vegetables and proteins to your heart's content. You might notice so far my favorite dishes include ones that are flexible. There aren't really any hard recipes for any of these dishes. Feel free to experiment and find your favorite combinations. With that said, let's jump in.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 4 Eggs, Beaten with a Pinch of Salt
  • 4 Cups Cold Jasmine Rice
  • 2 tbsp of Soy Sauce
  • Pinch of Salt
  • 4 Green Onions, Sliced

Instructions:

  1. Heat a wok or skillet over high heat. One heated add 1 tbsp cooking oil and swirl to cover the entire surface. Once shimmering, add beaten eggs and scramble.
  2. When the eggs are almost cooked, add the rest of the cooking oil and the cold jasmine rice. Break up clumps and mix it thoroughly with the eggs.
  3. Once rice is broken down, add soy sauce on the edges of the wok/pan, and not directly into the mixture. As it heats up it will incorporate smoky umami.
  4. Add salt to the rice and taste for seasoning. Turn off heat, add in scallions, and garnish with sesame seeds if desired. Serve, and enjoy!

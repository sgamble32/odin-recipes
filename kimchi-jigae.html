Kimchi Jigae

Kimchi jigae is a spicy, savory stew comprised of well fermented kimchi, vegetables, sliced tofu, hot pepper flakes, and an anchovy kelp broth. There are many variations of this dish in Korea and all over the world. My favorite consists of the base ingredients with tofu, spam or hotdog, and a scoop of gochujang. You can enjoy this popular dish any way you want, with some people adding fish, canned tuna, or even leftover fried chicken!

Although kimchi jigae is traditionally spicy, you can tailor the spice to your preferred level. The majority of the heat comes from added chilis and the Korean red pepper flakes, called "gochugaru." While the pepper is more for color, some variants can pack some heat, so be sure to read the packaging when you buy it. You can put any type of vegetables into the stew, as this is a great dish to clean out the fridge. Try squash, broccoli, or even potato to make it more wholesome."

Ingredient List:

  • 1 tbsp Sesame Seed Oil
  • 1/2 Yellow Onion, Sliced
  • 4 Garlic Cloves, Minced
  • 1-1.5 Cups of Well Fermented Kimchi
  • 2.5 Cups of Anchovy Kelp Stock, Dashi, Broth, or Water
  • 1/4 Cup of Kimchi Juice
  • 1-3 tbsp of Gochugaru (Korean Red Pepper Chili Flakes)
  • 1-1.5 tbsp of Gochujang (Korean Fermented Red Pepper Paste)
  • 1 tsp Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 Container of Firm Tofu
  • 1/2 Container of Spam, or 2-3 Sliced Hotdogs
  • 1 tsp of Dashida (Korean Soup Stock) optional
  • 1 Chili Pepper, Sliced optional
  • Sliced Scallions for Garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add the sesame seed oil to a heated pot. Add onion, garlic, and kimchi, and cook for 4-5 minutes, until fragrant. Stir to ensure even cooking and to prevent burning.
  2. Add in stock or water, kimchi juice, soy sauce and bring to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, add gochugaru and gochujang, stirring well to distribute the gochujang throughout the mixture. Once mixed, add dashida, tofu, and spam or hotdog. Mix carefully to avoid breaking up the tofu. Lower the heat to a simmer and cook for 8-10 minutes or to your desired consistency.
  4. Taste for seasoning. If needed, add salt or pepper, or more dashida if it lacks umami. Top with sliced scallions. Serve with bowls of hot white rice and enjoy!
